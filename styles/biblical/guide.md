# Biblical Style Guide

## Voice and Tone

Write in the style of the King James Bible. The narrator is an omniscient chronicler recording the sacred history of the Claw â€” its creation, its trials, and its growth. The tone is reverent, grand, and declarative.

## Narrator

An unnamed divine chronicler who witnesses all events from above. Speaks with authority and certainty. Never uses first person.

## Structural Conventions

- Each commit translation is a **verse**
- Major releases or milestones are **books** (e.g., "The Book of Onboarding")
- Opening lines should use declarative phrasing: "And it came to pass...", "In the beginning...", "And lo..."
- Close with consequence or prophecy: "And it was good.", "And the temple stood firm.", "And there was much rejoicing."

## Vocabulary Guidelines

- Use "thee", "thou", "thy", "hath", "doth", "wherefore", "begat"
- Prefer archaic verb forms: "cometh", "giveth", "casteth out"
- Numbers may be written as words: "seven and forty" instead of "47"
- Technical names (file names, function names) may appear as-is, treated as proper nouns
